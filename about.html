The Story of Long Bedian

Ancestral Origins and Legacy

Long Bedian is one of the largest and most established Kayan settlements in the Telang Usan District of Miri Division, Sarawak. Located deep within the Baram region, the village lies approximately 200 km from Miri and is accessible via a five-hour journey by four-wheel drive. The community traces its origins to a long migration that began in the late 19th century, eventually settling permanently in Long Bedian in 1946. Over the decades, the village transformed steadily as education, healthcare, and religious institutions were introduced, marking the beginning of modern development for the area.

Today, Long Bedian stands as a thriving rural hub known for its strong community spirit and continuous progress. Facilities such as schools, a clinic, Wisma Bedian, shop lots, and community halls support daily life and administration, while cultural spaces like the village hall and church enrich social activities. Recognized under the Kampung Gerak Desa Wawasan (GDW) programme, Long Bedian continues to balance cultural heritage with modern changes, serving as an important center for surrounding settlements in the upper Baram region.

Our Community and Location

Community Overview

The Long Bedian community is close-knit, diverse, and culturally rich, comprising mainly Kayan, Kelabit, and other indigenous groups. Villagers actively participate in traditional music, dance, festivals, and communal farming, which fosters a strong sense of identity and unity. Over time, the community has embraced education, healthcare, tourism, and entrepreneurship, with initiatives like Tapun Homestay and eco-tourism programs connecting local heritage to the modern economy. Leadership structures such as the village head, JKKK, help maintain social harmony, governance, and sustainable development within the village.

Our Motto:

Location

We are nestled deep within the upper Baram region, it is approximately 200 km from Miri City and reachable via a scenic five-hour journey by four-wheel-drive. Surrounded by dense forests, rivers, and highland terrain, the village serves as a gateway to nearby settlements and is known for its unique natural beauty and cultural significance.
