The Story of Long Bedian

Ancestral Origins and Legacy

Long Bedian's history is deeply intertwined with the **Baram River**, the ancestral highway for the Kayan people. The village was founded by early Kayan pioneers who migrated upstream, seeking fertile lands for farming and abundant fishing grounds. The current location was strategically chosen for its elevation, offering protection from floods and optimal access to the surrounding rainforest resources.

A significant moment in our history was the construction of the large modern longhouse, a symbol of community resilience and adaptation. It replaced older, smaller structures, signifying the unity and growth of the village in the modern era. Our founders established a foundation built on cooperation, respect for the river, and the enduring customs of the Orang Ulu.

Our Community and Location

Community Overview

Long Bedian is home to approximately **500 residents**, primarily composed of the **Kayan ethnic group**. We also welcome members of other Orang Ulu sub-groups. The village operates under the guidance of the Tuai Rumah (Longhouse Headman) and the Village Development and Security Committee (JKKK), ensuring traditional laws and modern governance work hand-in-hand.

Our Motto:

"Bersatu Teguh, Beradat Kukun" - Strong in Unity, Enduring in Custom.

Location

We are nestled deep in the interior of Sarawak, along the upper reaches of the **Baram River**, about halfway between Miri and Bario. While remote, access is typically via a mix of logging roads and longboat transport, a journey that offers breathtaking views of the Bornean rainforest.
